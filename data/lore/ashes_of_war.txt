Ashes of Warare specialitemsinELDEN RING(2022)that can be used to replace theSkillsof certainarmaments, and (optionally) infuse them with one of a selection of possibleAffinities. They can be applied to compatible armaments bySmithing Master Hewg; with anyWhetblade, they can also be applied while resting at aSite of Grace.[1][2]
Ashes of War are not consumed when applied; rather, they are slotted into the armament, and can be removed or transferred between compatible armaments at any time.
Each Ash of War corresponds to a specific Skill, and can only be applied to armaments compatible with that skill; for instance,Ash of War: Piercing Fangcan only be applied to weapons capable of thrusting attacks, butAsh of War: Kickcan be applied to any melee weapon. Ashes of War also cannot be applied to any weapon that is upgraded usingSomber Smithing Stones.
Ashes of War also each have a corresponding "native" Affinity; for instance,Ash of War: Bloody Slashhas theBlood affinity. When being applied to an armament, this Affinity can optionally be infused to change the weapon's base damage values, damage scaling, and/or status effect buildups; alternatively, the armament can be left with theStandard affinityto keep the default scaling, and other affinities can be chosen if correspondingWhetbladesare held.[note 1]
Ashes of War can be freely removed from weapons — refunding the Ash and reverting the weapon to its default Skill — or directly transferred between weapons. They can also be duplicated by Hewg withLost Ashes of War— whether in the inventory or already on a weapon — consuming them in the process.
Ashes of War are most commonly obtained as a drop fromAsh of War Scarabs; however, they are sometimes contained in chests, sold by merchants, dropped by certain enemies such asNight's Cavalryor the uniqueGodrick KnightinFort Haight, or occasionally are available as potential rewards from bossRemembrances— for example,Ash of War: Waves of Darknessfrom theRemembrance of the Naturalborn.
Additionally, some weapons have Ashes of War pre-equipped on acquisition, such that they can be applied to other weapons or duplicated from them. For example, theNagakibahas Ash of War: Piercing Fang equipped on acquisition, despite havingUnsheatheas its base skill when Piercing Fang is removed; andBackhand BladehasAsh of War: Blind Spotpre-equipped while simultaneously having Blind Spot as its underlying base skill. This often occurs when the Ash is otherwise impossible to obtain naturally.
It depends on the Ash of War individually if the Skill it grants scales with the weapon's attack rating, or your character's stats and the weapon's upgrades.
For example:Ash of War: Lion's Clawwill deal over 1000 damage on a Keen-infused +25Flambergeequipped to a character with 20str/40dex and less than 1000 if the Ash of War's default Heavy-infusion was chosen instead; meanwhile,Ash of War: Ground Slamwill deal less damage if any infusion option other than Heavy is chosen, even if the weapon's attack rating would be higher with something else.
