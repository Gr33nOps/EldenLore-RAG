Bell BearingsinElden RingareKey Itemswhich, when given to the enigmaticTwin Maiden Husksat theRoundtable Hold, expand their shop with new items. According to the lore of the Lands Between, a Bell Bearing represents the lingering essence and memories of commerce from the deceased. Though often collected after the tragic death ofNPCs, these macabre tokens are occasionally dropped byBosses— such as theBell Bearing Hunter, who relentlessly pursues merchants to claim these grisly prizes — or found abandoned throughout the world. Mechanically, only certainBell Bearingsare kept when going intoNew Game+. These include Bell Bearings that addcraftingorupgrade materialsbut do not include ones fromNPCsorMerchants.
This page covers their names, items they unlock, andLocations. In total, there are62Bell Bearings,26are kept in NG+.

Bell BearingsareKey Itemsthat can be given to theTwin Maiden Husksto expand their inventory.
Merchants and instructorsusually drop their respective Bell Bearings when they die, either from being killed by the player or from scripted events; when given to theTwin Maiden Husks, the bearing will grant access to the inventor the merchant had at the time of death. This means the potential yield gained from their bearings may vary greatly depending on how early or late the merchant has fallen in their journey.
Some Bell Bearings can be found scattered throughout theLands Between, or dropped by certain enemies such as theBell-Bearing Hunterupon defeat. They can also be found in theLand of Shadow, hidden in hanging jars  that must be destroyed with ranged attacks in order to release the items.
